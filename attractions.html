Our Attractions

NASA Zone

Re-live the hayday of NASA and step back in to the 1980's when NASA still made and flew things. NASA Zone offers a historical perspective of the good old days when cars would kill you and cigarettes were a great idea. See inside a full-size model of the Space Shuttle and try on a real space suite made for the famous shuttle Hubble Space Telescope EVA. Stand underneath a used Apollo-era Saturn 5 rocket engine and feel the vibration as it starts up!

SpaceX Zone

Your adventure begins with a real SpaceX launch (every day at 3pm) taking people to Sydney, Australia in only 26 minutes. The fun continues as the incoming return flight lands at 3:30pm direct from SpaceX's offshore launch facility in Sydney. From there, explore the SpaceX operation center where the rockets are scheduled and controled. Watch real SpaceX employees press buttons and fiddle with knobs to launch the rocket!

Black Holes Zone

Fall in to your own black hole at our VR physics simulator. You might want to wait for lunch in case you lose it! Our black hole simulator is so real it includes real blackness brought from space! You can see the other side of the black hole thanks to our relativistically accurate simulation engine. Measure the hole size using our Schwartzschild radius measuring stick. But don't worry, you're safely on Earth the whole time thanks to the magic of VR!

Searching For Extraterrestrial Life

Aliens are already here on Earth, you can see them any night in their UFOs. But if they weren't, then our SETI area is the next best thing! Look for little green men through our phased array radio telescope and examing Martian becterial samples returned from Mars Base 1. Where did the bacteria come from? Explore these mysteries and more at the SETI exhibit!

Mars Exploration

So you want to go to Mars but can't afford the ticket? Out 10,000 sq. m. enclosed MarsScape simulator includes real Martian sand and a replica of the original Mars Base 1 entrance port. But don't worry! You won't need your spacesuit as you explore this foreign world and all it's mysteries. There's even dinosaur bones to uncover near the base to confuse and entertain the budding paleontologist.

International Space Station

The ISS may have blown up during the First Alien War, but you can relive its glory inside a full scale, 100% accurate model. Put on your spacesuit and go underwater (simulating no gravity) and float around this marvel of engineering. The FakeISS even includes a reconstructed pergola unit with breathtaking views of the Earth as it was before we defeated the Alien Hordes.

Experimental Zone

Build real experiments using laser, neutron beams and anything else you can find in our reclaimed nuclear weapons laboratory! Only at SpacePark can you find the mass of the positron using readily-available equipment, all certified by the National Science Foundation!

Movies Zone

Movies play an important part in space exploration, from 2001 to Aliens 3 they have continually helped the public imagine and feel the magic of space. In Movie Zone you can argue with a real HAL 9000 computer - can you get it to close the pod bay doors? Escape an alien nest and shoot real predators in our space colony simulator.

Movie Theater

Ready to be sucked in to space? Watch a 3D tour unfold showing the greatest moments in space exploration. Sit with Yuri Gagarin as he orbits the Eath for the first time. Ride along on the first Space Shuttle launch and then plunge in the depths of the universe as you explore a guided tour of the nearest 10 solar systems.

Simulators Zone

Astronaut Training

Learn to float like a real astronaut in our zero-g training harnesses. Your simulated mission begins by exiting the air lock in to the vast inky blackness of space. From there, your task is to repair the pod bay doors using special space spanners. Can you do it in time? The clock is ticking to save the space station and return safely to Earth, all the while guided by our expert astronaut trainers.

Pilot The Spaceship

Fly an original NASA Space Shuttle simulator to the stars. You and three friends will launch, fly and land the closest thing to a real Space Shuttle. Talk to mission control and work your assigned mission tasks one by one as you push the boundaries of spaceflight. But wait, there's more! Open the bay doors and get ready to accept a 100% accurate communications satellite that needs repairs. Fix it quickly before returning to a safe landing at Kennedy Space Center!

Low Gravity Walk

What does low gravity feel like? How far can you jump? We can simulate Moon or Mars gravity instantly at the Low Gravity Walk. You'll be doing somersaults 30 feet in the air in the safe hands of our gravity experts. Want to feel how heavy it is to walk on Jupiter? For a small additional fee we can make you weigh like one million killigrams to give you just a taste of what extreme gravity can feel like!
